Where were the last names obtained?

The surnames were obtained from the Argentine electoral register corresponding to the year 2015.
Indicators

Unbiased random isonymy (INS)

Isonymy is the occurrence of the same surname in two individuals.

According to Relethford (1988) when all the individuals within a population have different last names, the isonymy should be equal to 0.

Based on the distribution of the last names in each department or province, the Random Isonymy (INS) was determined, according to Morton (1973).

Fisher's alpha

This indicator measures the effective number of surnames in a population. Piazza et al. (1987) used it for the first time to analyze migration rates from the distribution of surnames. High values are observed in expanding populations, where there is a significant flow of immigrants, while low values occur in isolated communities with high gene drift.

In this work, the formula of Barrai et al. (1992) was used.

Inbreeding estimator

The inbreeding coefficient was calculated from a derivation of the Crow and Mange formula, which represents inbreeding as an expression of the isonymy of random unions or unbiased bias.

Inbreeding estimator = INS / 4

Index A

Indicator A represents the percentage of the population that is the sole bearer of their last name.

In the maps, the indicator can take values between 0 and 1, so a value 0.1 represents that 10% of the population of the province or department is a carrier of their last name.

Index B

Indicator B the percentage of the population whose last name is included in the 7 most frequent last names of the population

In the maps, it can take values between 0 and 1, so a value of 0.1 represents that 10% of the population of the province or department carries one of the 7 most popular surnames.

Kiviat diagram (radar graph)

Each axis of the graph is normalized between 0 and 1 using the maximum and minimum values of each indicator in the entire data (that is, using the values of each indicator for all provinces).

The graph was divided into four parts.

Distribution

Surname used

For each person in the register, the first surname in his/her family name was used to consult its distribution.
